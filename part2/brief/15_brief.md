## 15.1 The Reduction Program

The GSF Lagrangian and EOM are structurally invariant (same form at all organizational levels):

$$\mathcal{L} = \|\dot\Gamma\|_F^2 - \|\Gamma\|_F^2 - 2\!\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{\mathrm{GR}}}\right)^{\!2}\det(\Gamma)$$

$$\ddot\Gamma + \gamma\dot\Gamma + \Gamma + \left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{\mathrm{GR}}}\right)^{\!2}\mathrm{adj}(\Gamma)^T = N(t)$$

Ch15 carries out the **reduction program**: assign attributes $\{S, A, \mathbf{I}, \mathbf{R}\}$, impose domain constraints on $\Gamma$, evaluate the EOM, verify against established equations. Five physical domains; zero new parameters.

**Terminological clarification (UoC vs regime).** Each reduction identifies a **dynamical regime** of an underlying UoC, not a new UoC. The UoC remains the entity (mass, charged system, fluid parcel); the rank-$\leq 2$ reductions characterize the regime in which certain attributes are momentarily inactive. This is consistent with Ch12 §12.3.2 (boundary configurations are not UoCs in the full sense). Wave-particle duality is therefore a duality of *regimes* of the same underlying UoC — see Remark 15.8.

## 15.2 Central Structural Tool — Lemma 15.1

**Lemma 15.1.** If $\Gamma$ is a $4\times 4$ matrix with $\mathrm{rank}(\Gamma) \leq 2$, then $\mathrm{adj}(\Gamma) = 0$.

*Proof.* Each entry of $\mathrm{adj}(\Gamma)$ is a $3\times3$ cofactor. If rank $\leq 2$, every $3\times3$ submatrix contains a zero row — cofactor = 0. $\square$

This lemma is the key to all linear reductions. When $S = A = 0$, the coupling matrix has rank $\leq 2$ and the nonlinear attractor term vanishes identically.

## 15.3 Five Reductions

### Scalar (1×1)

$\Gamma = (x)$: EOM becomes $\ddot u + \gamma\dot u + u = 0$ where $u = x + (\rho_\star/\rho_{\mathrm{GR}})^2$.

**Proposition 15.1.** The structural equilibrium is $x^\star = -(\rho_\star/\rho_{\mathrm{GR}})^2$ — real, negative, outside $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{adm}} = \{x > 0\}$. No stable interior attractor exists for the 1D scalar. Consistent with Ch14: matter requires crossing the Lorentzian potential barrier.

### Simple Harmonic Oscillator

Null-purpose embedding: $S = A = 0$, physical content in $(\mathbf{I},\mathbf{R})$ block:

$$\Gamma = \mathrm{diag}_{SA=0}\begin{pmatrix} 0 & x \\ -x & 0 \end{pmatrix}_{\mathbf{IR}}$$

By Lemma 15.1, $\mathrm{adj}(\Gamma) = 0$. EOM gives:

$$\boxed{\ddot x + \gamma\dot x + x = 0} \quad\checkmark$$

**Theorem 15.1.** Exact — zero parameter adjustment. The null $S, A$ attributes encode the absence of charge and agency.

**Remark 15.3.** Linear physics is exact when $\mathrm{rank} \leq 2$. Any system developing nonzero $S$ or $A$ — however small — exits the linear regime. Observed linearity is a consequence of organizational simplicity, not a fundamental property.

### Pendulum

**Status: geometric analogy, not derivation.** Geodesics on $\|\Gamma\|_F = r$ are great circles — no restoring force. The pendulum equation $\ddot\theta + \sin\theta = 0$ requires (i) compact $S^1$ configuration space and (ii) gravitational potential $V(\theta) = gL(1-\cos\theta)$ — domain-specific inputs outside the present Lagrangian. Deferred to Part III.

**Why the GSF Lagrangian cannot derive the pendulum — precise statement.** The failure is geometric, not physical. The GSF lives on $\mathrm{GL}(4,\mathbb{R})$ — an open, non-compact manifold. For a scalar system ($1\times 1$), $\mathrm{adj}(\Gamma) = 1$ (constant), so the EOM reduces to a shifted harmonic oscillator, not a pendulum. The $\sin\theta$ restoring force requires (a) the compact orbit $S^1 \subset \mathrm{GL}(4,\mathbb{R})$ generated by the SO(4) action on $P(\Gamma)$, and (b) a coupling to the gravitational sector ($\Gamma_s \sim g_{\mu\nu}$, Ch31) that produces the gravitational potential energy $gL(1-\cos\theta)$. Neither is available within the current Lagrangian restricted to $\mathrm{GL}(4,\mathbb{R})$ without bundle structure.

**Part III roadmap (B4).** Two steps: (1) identify $\theta$ as the angle parametrizing the SO(4)-orbit of $P(\Gamma)$ — a compact submanifold of $\mathcal{M}(\Gamma)$; (2) derive $V(\theta)$ as a cross-coupling term $\rho_{\mathrm{grav}} \cdot gL(1-\cos\theta)$ from the gravitational reduction of Ch31–Ch32. The pendulum emerges as the dimensional reduction of the GSF dynamics to the compact orbit, coupled to the gravitational sector.


### Electromagnetic Vacuum — Curvature Formulation

**Attribute assignment:** $S = A = 0$ (vacuum), $\Gamma = \Gamma_a = F_{\mu\nu}$ (antisymmetric, $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ in $\mathbf{I},\mathbf{R}$ sector).

**Adjugate chain:** transverse EM $\implies$ $\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B} = 0 \implies \mathrm{Pf}(F) = 0 \implies \det(F) = 0$. For antisymmetric $4\times4$, rank must be even; $\det = 0$ eliminates rank 4, so $\mathrm{rank}(F) \leq 2$. By Lemma 15.1: $\mathrm{adj}(F) = 0$.

**Proposition 15.3 (Antisymmetric sector as curvature).** $\Gamma_a \in \Lambda^2V$ — not a primary variable but a curvature 2-form $\Gamma_a = F = dA$. Consequences:

| | Consequence | Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Bianchi | $dF = d^2A = 0$ | $d^2 = 0$ |
| Maxwell | $\partial_\mu F^{\mu\nu} = 0$ | $\delta\|dA\|^2/\delta A = 0$ |
| Gauge | $A \to A + d\Lambda$ redundancy | $d^2\Lambda = 0$ |

**Mass protection.** $\Gamma_s$ (primary tensor) admits mass: $\|\Gamma_s\|_F^2$ is a mass term. $\Gamma_a = dA$ (curvature) is massless: $\|dA\|_F^2$ is kinetic for $A$, not a mass term. Masslessness is a representation consequence, not an imposed symmetry.

**Theorem 15.2.** $\Gamma_a = F = dA$ with $\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B} = 0$ yields both sets of Maxwell equations. No gauge patching. *(Bundle embedding deferred to Part III.)*

$$\boxed{\partial_\mu F^{\mu\nu} = 0, \quad \partial_{[\mu}F_{\nu\rho]} = 0} \quad\checkmark$$

### Linearized Gravity

**Attribute assignment:** $\Gamma_s = \eta_{\mu\nu} + h_{\mu\nu}$, $\Gamma_a = 0$.

Linearizing $\mathrm{adj}(\Gamma_s)^T$ at first order in $h$, with $\rho = \rho_{\mathrm{GR}}$ (so $\mu = 2$):

$$\square h_{\mu\nu} + \underbrace{(1+1)}_{m_h^2}h_{\mu\nu} - \eta_{\mu\nu}\mathrm{Tr}(h) = 0$$

The mass: $m_h^2 = 2 - \mu(\rho_{\mathrm{GR}}) = 2 - 2 = 0$. In de Donder gauge:

$$\boxed{\square h_{\mu\nu} = 0} \quad\checkmark$$

**Theorem 15.3.** The massless graviton is an exact consequence of $\mu(\rho_{\mathrm{GR}}) = 2$, derived independently in Ch13 from the normalization anchor $c(\rho_{\mathrm{GR}}) = 1$.

## 15.6 Espectro de Modos — Análisis Lineal (Ch15b)

Linealizar el EOM alrededor de un fondo homogéneo $\Gamma_0 = \sigma \cdot I$ con $\sigma > 0$ produce el operador de masa efectiva:

$$\mathcal{M}_{\Gamma_0}[\delta\Gamma] = \delta\Gamma + \mu\det(\Gamma_0)\left[\mathrm{Tr}(\Gamma_0^{-1}\delta\Gamma)\,\Gamma_0^{-T} - \Gamma_0^{-T}\delta\Gamma^T\Gamma_0^{-T}\right]$$

Para $\Gamma_0 = \sigma I$, los modos se clasifican en tres sectores con autovalores exactos:

| Sector | Modo | Autovalor $\lambda$ |
|---|---|---|
| Traza ($\delta\Gamma \propto I$) | Escalado uniforme | $\lambda_{\mathrm{tr}} = 1 + 3\mu\sigma^2$ |
| Simétrico sin traza | Deformación de forma | $\lambda_{\mathrm{sym}} = 1 - \mu\sigma^2$ |
| Antisimétrico | Campo gauge | $\lambda_{\mathrm{anti}} = 1 + \mu\sigma^2$ |

**Clasificación espectral:** $\lambda = 0$ (propagante), $\lambda > 0$ (ligado), $\lambda < 0$ (inestable).

**Punto crítico espectral.** $\lambda_{\mathrm{sym}} = 0$ ocurre en $\sigma^\star = \sqrt{1/\mu} = \rho_{\mathrm{GR}}/\rho$.

**Theorem 15b.1 (Umbral espectral = umbral de creación de materia).** El punto $\sigma^\star$ donde $\lambda_{\mathrm{sym}} = 0$ coincide exactamente con el umbral de creación de materia $\sigma^\star = \rho_{\mathrm{GR}}/\rho$ de Ch14 §14.8.1. Mismo cálculo desde dos perspectivas independientes.

**Proposition 15b.1 (El atractor es la transición de fase).** El atractor $P(\Gamma) \sim \sigma^\star I$ es equivalente, vía rotación $O(4)$, al punto donde el sector simétrico sin traza se hace sin masa — la transición de fase espectral.

**Retrato espectral:**

| Régimen | $\lambda_{\mathrm{tr}}$ | $\lambda_{\mathrm{sym}}$ | $\lambda_{\mathrm{anti}}$ | Física |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\sigma < \sigma^\star$ | $> 1$ | $> 0$ | $> 1$ | Todos los modos estables; sin materia |
| $\sigma = \sigma^\star$ | $4$ | $0$ | $2$ | Modo simétrico sin masa = umbral de creación |

**Conexión con los regímenes de Ch15.** El sector antisimétrico tiene $\lambda_{\mathrm{anti}} = 1 + \mu\sigma^2 > 0$ siempre — consistente con la protección de masa de $\Gamma_a = dA$ (ningún modo antisimétrico se hace inestable). El sector simétrico sin traza cruza cero en $\sigma^\star$ — este es el mecanismo dinámico detrás de la bifurcación onda-partícula de Remark 15.8.
