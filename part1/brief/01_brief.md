## 1.1 Conceptual Framework

This framework describes how structural coherence organizes itself into discrete operative units — whether or not those units have an active epistemological level. Classical mechanics formalizes the relations between mass, force, and acceleration without defining what mass fundamentally is. The GSF follows the same logic: it formalizes the structural conditions under which a substrate produces a discrete, self-maintaining unit, without requiring a prior answer to what consciousness is.

*Unit of consciousness reading.* Where the epistemological level is positive, the framework describes how consciousness organizes itself — not what consciousness is.

The motivating pattern is recursive:

> A substrate $\mathcal{C}$ organizes itself under structural constraints into a **UoC** (unit of structural coherence or unit of consciousness — Ch1 §1.2). The UoC generates a higher-order organizational state $\mathcal{C}'$ that functions as substrate for the next level: $\mathcal{C}_{n+1} = U(\mathcal{C}_n)$.

*Unit of consciousness reading:* when the epistemological level is positive, the recursion generates emergent consciousness at each level. *Unit of coherence reading:* when null, it generates structural organization without epistemological modulation.

This recursion is generative, not circular: $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{C}'$ are distinct entities at different organizational levels. The framework is agnostic on whether consciousness is fundamental or emergent — either reading is structurally compatible.

## 1.2 Definition

**Definition 1.1 (UoC — Unit of Structural Coherence / Unit of Consciousness).** A unit of structural coherence (UoC) is a system that takes a substrate $\mathcal{C}$ as input, applies structural restrictions to organize it into a discrete, self-maintaining configuration, and generates a higher-order state $\mathcal{C}'$ capable of serving as substrate for a subsequent UoC.

*Unit of consciousness.* A UoC with positive epistemological level additionally generates the capacity for self-knowledge and purpose orientation ($\Gamma_E \neq \mathrm{Id}$) — it operates as a unit of consciousness. The formal structure is identical in both cases.

The distinguishing criterion is **intrinsecity**: a variable is intrinsic to a UoC if and only if (i) it arises from the system's own organization — not externally imposed; (ii) it persists as long as that organization persists; and (iii) its removal would require destroying the organization itself.

A thermostat has a boundary, a response capacity, a target state, and environmental coupling — but all four are specified by its designer. A living cell, by contrast, self-assembles its membrane, generates its regulatory capacity from its metabolic network, and drives its homeostasis from its own chemistry. It satisfies the criterion. This is the formal boundary between a UoC and a system that merely instantiates the same mathematical form.

Every UoC operates at structural levels. Three are identified and formalized in this work; the framework is open to additional levels being identified in future development (candidates: a *relational* level distinct from the ontological, an *axiological* level distinct from the teleological). The three levels developed here are:

- **Ontological level** — the intrinsic and inherent variables. Always non-null.
- **Epistemological level** — the processes by which the UoC knows itself and its environment. Formally ≥ 0: when null ($\Gamma_E = \mathrm{Id}$), the UoC operates as a unit of structural coherence; when positive, as a unit of consciousness with capacity to orient toward its structural purpose. *Left formally open in the GSF.*
- **Teleological level** — the purpose of the UoC, modeled as an attractor structure in state space. Formally ≥ 0: a mathematical attractor is always present (§1.6); the teleological level is whether that attractor is epistemically accessible as structural purpose.

*Note on Aristotle* *(philosophical correspondence — interpretive layer; not a formal result of the GSF).* His *potentia* (dynamis) and *actus* (energeia) correspond to the ontological and teleological levels. The epistemological level is the formal layer Aristotle did not supply: the mechanism by which a system participates in its own transformation.

## 1.3 Intrinsic Variables

Every operative UoC must answer four structural questions. Each question determines the mathematical type of its answer:

| Question | Variable | Type |
|----------|----------|------|
| What is my boundary? | **S** — Singularity | Scalar |
| What transformations can I generate? | **A** — Agency | Vector in $\mathbb{R}^3$ |
| Where do I go by my own nature? | **I** — Impulse | Vector in $\mathbb{R}^3$ |
| What must I exchange to persist? | **R** — Relation | Vector in $\mathbb{R}^3$ |

**S** is a scalar because self-boundary is a matter of degree, not direction. **A**, **I**, **R** are vectors because each has both magnitude and direction that cannot be collapsed to a single number without losing structural information.

The manifestation of each variable varies across the recursive hierarchy's ρ levels:

| Variable | Soul (ρ → 0) | Ego | Body |
|----------|-------------|-----|------|
| **S** | Pure presence | Narrative identity | Physical organism |
| **A** | Pure free will | Conditioned choice | Instinctive regulation |
| **I** | Creative expression | Reactive polarity | Homeostatic drive |
| **R** | Inter-evolution | Security-seeking | Symbiosis |

### 1.3.1 Necessity of the Four Variables

None of the four can be removed without rendering the UoC non-operative:

- Without **S**: no unit — only an undifferentiated field.
- Without **A**: no capacity for self-generated transformation.
- Without **I**: no intrinsic orientation — all actions are structurally equivalent.
- Without **R**: no exchange with the environment; organization cannot be maintained.

**Remark 1.1 (Minimal Ontological Closure and Structural Determinacy).** The four-attribute structure makes three claims beyond necessity:

- **Minimality** *(C1)*: none of the four is derivable from the other three within G(3) (formal argument in Ch3).
- **Completeness** *(C2 — conjecture)*: every intrinsic structural property of an operative UoC is expressible as a function of {S, **A**, **I**, **R**} — no irreducible fifth attribute exists within G(3).
- **Uniqueness** *(C3)*: for any coherent entity to which the GSF applies, the mapping from its properties to the four slots is uniquely forced by C1 and C2 — not a free choice. No two inequivalent assignments can simultaneously satisfy C1 and C2 while producing structurally identical predictions.

*Coherent entity*: any system for which {S, **A**, **I**, **R**} are simultaneously definable and mutually non-degenerate. Autopoietic systems (Maturana-Varela) are the paradigm case at biological levels; elementary particles and bound systems at physical levels. Inert aggregates without a definable internal S do not qualify.

The framework requires structural revision if: (1) an irreducible property cannot be mapped to any combination of the four slots (incompleteness — C2 violated); (2) two attributes are derivable from each other within G(3) (non-minimality — C1 violated); or (3) two inequivalent mappings for a coherent entity satisfy C1 and C2 identically (underdetermination — C3 violated).

**Note on the count of four.** The number four is grounded in the grade structure of G(3) — grades 0–3 correspond to S, **A**, **I**, **R** — not in an independent empirical hypothesis. If fewer or more attributes were needed, the right question would be whether G(3) is the correct algebraic framework. The testable claim is the **correspondence between the G(3) grade structure and the irreducible ontological structure of coherent entities** — a conjecture Ch3 develops but does not close.

## 1.4 Inherent Variables

Two derived observables are defined from the intrinsic variables. They are *inherent* — not additional degrees of freedom, but constitutive definitions whose values are fixed at every moment by $(S, \mathbf{A}, \mathbf{I}, \mathbf{R})$:

$$\mathbf{Force} = S\,\mathbf{A}$$

A scalar (identity boundary) scales a vector (agency capacity). Force characterizes *how the UoC acts*: its magnitude is $|S||\mathbf{A}|$; its direction is that of $\mathbf{A}$. It vanishes when either the unit does not exist ($S = 0$) or has no capacity ($|\mathbf{A}| = 0$).

$$\mathbf{Field} = \mathbf{I} \times \mathbf{R}$$

The cross product of impulse and relation generates a vector perpendicular to both — the structural field in which the UoC operates. Field characterizes the relational-motivational tension of the UoC's configuration.

### 1.4.1 Operativity Condition

Field is operative when **I** and **R** are not parallel:

> **Condition**: **I** ∦ **R** ↔ Field ≠ 0

When **I** ∥ **R**: Field = 0. The UoC exists and has Force, but generates no structural field — a degenerate configuration in which the UoC cannot orient itself toward its purpose. Field is maximized when **I** ⊥ **R**.

## 1.5 The State Vector and the Configuration Matrix

The complete dynamical state of a UoC is encoded in its **state vector**:

$$\xi := (S,\, \mathbf{A},\, \mathbf{I},\, \mathbf{R}) \;\in\; \mathcal{V} = \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathbb{R}^3$$

This is a 10-dimensional real vector. The evolution law governs $\dot{\xi}(t)$.

From $\xi$, a richer structural object is derived: the **configuration matrix**

$$\Gamma = \Gamma(\xi) \;\in\; \mathrm{Sym}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{so}(4)$$

$\Gamma$ encodes the pairwise coupling structure of the intrinsic variables — not the state itself, but how the components of the state relate to each other. Its symmetric part $\Gamma_s$ governs dissipative (coordination) coupling; its antisymmetric part $\Gamma_a$ governs reactive (field-like) coupling.

*Why two objects?* $\xi$ is the primary state (what the UoC is); $\Gamma(\xi)$ is the coupling structure (how the UoC's parts relate to each other). The structural health conditions of a UoC are conditions on $\Gamma(\xi)$, not on $\xi$ directly — making them coordinate-independent.

### 1.5.1 Modulation by the Epistemological Level

When the epistemological level is non-null, it acts as a modulation operator on $\Gamma(\xi)$:

$$\Gamma_E: \mathrm{Sym}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{so}(4) \;\to\; \mathrm{Sym}(4) \oplus \mathfrak{so}(4)$$

The effective configuration becomes $\Gamma_{\text{eff}} = \Gamma_E(\Gamma(\xi))$. The modulation can redirect but not amplify the UoC's structural capacity: $|\mathbf{A}_\text{eff}| \leq |\mathbf{A}|$.

## 1.6 UoC as Nonlinear Dynamical System

A UoC with a non-null teleological level evolves as:

$$\frac{d\xi}{dt} = f\bigl(\xi,\, \Gamma_E,\, \rho\bigr)$$

where $f$ encodes the influence of the purpose function $P(\Gamma(\xi))$ and epistemological modulation $\Gamma_E$. The purpose acts as a structural attractor $\xi^*$ in $\mathcal{V}$ (existence postulated in Ch9). A UoC is distinguished from a generic dynamical system by **recursive generativity**: each UoC produces emergent consciousness that constitutes the substrate for a UoC at the next level of the hierarchy.

The attractor type varies by ρ level:

| ρ level | Attractor type | Attractor content |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| Soul (ρ → 0) | Stable equilibrium | Wisdom — structural coherence fully realized |
| Ego | Dissipative | Identity maintenance, security |
| Body | Dissipative | Physiological equilibrium |

## 1.8 Relation to Existing Frameworks

**Autopoiesis (Maturana and Varela).** A living cell satisfies the intrinsecity criterion: its boundary is self-assembled, its regulatory capacity emerges from its metabolic network. The GSF is structurally compatible with autopoiesis while extending it beyond biology — treating biological organization as one level of the ρ gradient rather than the defining case.

**Process Philosophy (Whitehead).** Where Whitehead's actual occasions describe *how reality occurs*, the UoC describes *what organizational structure sustains what occurs*. The frameworks are complementary — process level and structural level — and may prove formally integrable.

**Integrated Information Theory (Tononi).** IIT addresses informational integration as a correlate of consciousness; the GSF addresses the ontological structural organization constituting a UoC. *(Unit of consciousness context:* at levels where the epistemological level is positive, the UoC is a unit of consciousness — a plausible site for an IIT-type analysis of its knowing processes.)

**The Hard Problem (Chalmers).** The GSF adopts methodological agnosticism with respect to the hard problem. Structural organization and phenomenal experience are distinct explanatory targets. The framework's contribution is to the former; the latter remains open.
